MY HONEST THOUGHTS ABOUT AUSTRALIAN HOTEL CASINOS

PUBLISHED: 04 OCTOBER 2025

so like ive been thinking about this heaps lately yeah because my family stayed at this hotel casino in queensland for a weekend and it was pretty different from what i expected tbh. like before we went i thought it would be all fancy and posh and stuff but when we actually got there it was kinda more casual than that. dont get me wrong it wasnt bad or anything just different from the movies i guess lol.

the whole vibe of these places is interesting because theyre trying to be hotels AND casinos at the same time which seems hard to do properly. like some bits feel really upmarket with nice rooms and restaurants but then other parts are just full of pokies machines and people in tracksuits having a punt. its like two different worlds mashed together and sometimes it works and sometimes it feels a bit weird honestly.

i reckon theres heaps to talk about when it comes to whether these places are actually worth staying at or if youre better off just going to a regular hotel. ive got opinions about the rooms the food the casino bit itself and whether the whole package makes sense for different types of people. so yeah lets get into it properly.

after spending like three days there and watching how everything works ive noticed some patterns that people should probably know about before they book. some things are genuinely good value and other things are kinda overpriced when you think about it. ill try to break it all down as honest as i can yeah.

THE ROOMS ARE ACTUALLY PRETTY DECENT MOST OF THE TIME

okay so the rooms were probably the best part of the whole experience if im being real. our room was on like the eighth floor and it was way bigger than i thought it would be. had a proper king bed nice bathroom with a good shower and the aircon worked really well which is important in queensland obviously. the view wasnt amazing or anything just looked out at the carpark but the room itself was clean and comfortable.

what i liked was that everything felt pretty new or at least well maintained. like the tv was decent size the wifi actually worked properly and there was heaps of power points for charging stuff. the minibar was expensive as but thats normal for any hotel i reckon. they had a little coffee machine thing which was good for mornings before breakfast.

the beds were comfortable enough that i actually slept really well even though you could hear some noise from the casino downstairs sometimes. they give you like multiple pillows and the sheets felt nice quality. bathroom had good water pressure and plenty of hot water. honestly for the price we paid which was around two hundred a night i reckon the rooms are fair value compared to other hotels ive stayed at.

THINGS THAT COULD BE BETTER WITH THE ROOMS

but like there were some annoying things too yeah. the walls are pretty thin so you can hear people in the hallway talking and doors slamming which got old pretty quick. also our room had this weird smell when we first walked in like cleaning chemicals mixed with cigarette smoke even though it was supposedly a non smoking room. it went away after we opened the window for a bit but still.

another thing is the rooms dont have much personality or style. theyre just kind of generic hotel room vibes with boring beige colors and basic furniture. i guess thats not a huge deal but when youre paying decent money it would be nice if they made it feel a bit more special or unique. some of the furniture looked a bit worn out too like the desk chair was wobbly and had marks on it.

THE CASINO FLOOR IS OVERWHELMING BUT INTERESTING

right so obviously the main thing that makes these places different from normal hotels is the casino bit. im only sixteen so i couldnt actually gamble or anything but i walked through it heaps of times to get to different parts of the hotel and its definitely an experience. the noise is crazy loud with all the pokies machines beeping and playing music and people talking everywhere.

what surprised me was how many older people were there just sitting at the machines for ages. like some people looked like they barely moved for hours which seemed pretty sad honestly. but then there were also groups of younger people at the card tables having fun and laughing so i guess different people use it different ways. the whole atmosphere is pretty intense though with lots of flashing lights and that distinct casino smell of carpet and air freshener.

from what my parents said the minimum bets were pretty reasonable like you could play the pokies for small amounts if you wanted to just have a quick go. the card tables had higher minimums especially at night when it got busier. they have all the usual stuff like blackjack roulette poker tables and heaps of different slot machines. security was everywhere too which i guess makes sense with all the money floating around.

IS THE CASINO PART ACTUALLY FUN THOUGH

this is where it gets complicated because it depends heaps on what youre into. if you like gambling then yeah obviously having a casino right there in your hotel is super convenient. you can just wander down in casual clothes any time and have a go without having to travel anywhere. my dad said it was good to be able to play a few hands of blackjack after dinner without making a big deal out of it.

but if youre not into gambling then the casino is kind of just this big noisy space you have to walk through to get places which gets annoying. like to get from our room to the restaurant we had to go through the casino floor and the smoke and noise was pretty full on especially in the morning when you just want breakfast. i reckon they should have better routes around the casino for people who dont want to be in that environment.

FOOD OPTIONS ARE MIXED QUALITY AND VALUE

the hotel had like three different restaurants plus a cafe and a bar that did food. we tried most of them over the weekend and honestly the quality varied heaps. the main restaurant was pretty good with decent steaks and pasta and stuff but it was expensive like forty dollars for a main course. the portions were big though so i guess you get what you pay for.

the buffet breakfast was included in our room rate which was good value actually. they had heaps of options like cooked breakfast cereals fruit pastries and the coffee was alright. it got really busy though so you had to get there early or wait ages for a table. the food was kept pretty fresh and hot which is important for buffets because sometimes they can be gross.

we also tried the casual dining place near the casino which was more like pub food burgers and pizzas and that. it was okay nothing special but convenient if you just wanted something quick. prices were reasonable for hotel food like fifteen to twenty dollars for a meal. the pizza was actually pretty decent surprisingly and came out fast.

COMPARING PRICES TO OUTSIDE RESTAURANTS

this is where hotel casinos can be a bit annoying because youre kind of a captive audience. like if you dont have a car or cant be bothered leaving the hotel youre stuck with their food options and prices. we looked at some restaurants nearby and they were definitely cheaper for similar quality food. but then again the convenience of eating in the hotel is worth something i guess especially if youve been drinking at the casino.

my advice would be to eat breakfast at the hotel if its included then maybe go out for lunch or dinner to save money and get better value. or bring snacks and drinks with you to keep in the room because the minibar and room service are crazy expensive. like they wanted eight dollars for a small bottle of coke which is just ridiculous tbh.

OTHER FACILITIES AND AMENITIES WORTH KNOWING ABOUT

most hotel casinos have other stuff besides just rooms and gambling which can make them better value. this place had a decent sized pool which was nice even though it was pretty busy. the gym was small but had enough equipment if you wanted to work out. there was also a spa but we didnt use it because it looked expensive from the prices we saw.

they had entertainment too like live music in the bar on friday and saturday nights which was actually pretty good. some local band was playing and they were talented. no cover charge either you just had to buy drinks. the casino also had some kind of rewards program where you could earn points for gambling that gave you discounts on food and rooms which seems smart if youre a regular.

parking was free which is good because some hotels charge heaps for that. wifi was included too and worked well enough for streaming and stuff. the lobby had a business center with computers and a printer if you needed that. overall the facilities were pretty standard for a mid range hotel nothing amazing but everything you actually need.

WHO SHOULD ACTUALLY STAY AT THESE PLACES

after experiencing it i reckon hotel casinos are good for certain types of people but not everyone. if you like gambling obviously then its perfect because you can play whenever you want without leaving. couples who want a weekend away with some entertainment options would probably enjoy it too. the combination of decent rooms food options and activities all in one place is convenient.

but i dont think theyre great for families with little kids because the casino environment is pretty adult focused and you cant really avoid it. also if youre looking for a quiet relaxing getaway this probably isnt it because theres constant noise and activity. people who dont drink or gamble might feel a bit out of place too since thats clearly what the place is designed around.

for business travelers i reckon it depends on the location and facilities. if the hotel casino is in a good spot and has meeting rooms and stuff then yeah it could work. but the casino atmosphere might be distracting if youre trying to focus on work. regular hotels are probably better for business trips unless you specifically want the casino entertainment after meetings.

VALUE FOR MONEY COMPARED TO REGULAR HOTELS

this is the big question right like are you actually getting better value by staying at a hotel casino versus a normal hotel. from what i saw the room prices were pretty similar to other mid range hotels in the area. so youre not really saving money just by choosing a hotel casino. where you might get value is if you use the facilities and entertainment options.

if you were planning to go out to restaurants bars and entertainment anyway then having it all in one place could save you money on transport and make things more convenient. plus the rewards programs can add up if you visit regularly. but if you just want a place to sleep and shower then a regular hotel is probably better value because youre not paying extra for casino facilities you wont use.

THINGS I WISH I KNEW BEFORE BOOKING

there are definitely some things that would have been good to know before we booked this place. first off the noise levels are higher than regular hotels especially if your room is near the casino floor or elevators. ask for a room on a higher floor away from the action if you want better sleep. also weekends are way busier than weekdays so if you want a quieter experience go during the week.

check what facilities are actually included in your room rate because some things that seem like they should be free actually cost extra. like the gym was free but towels for the pool cost five dollars which seemed petty. room service had a delivery fee on top of the already expensive food prices. these little extras add up quick if youre not careful.

also look at reviews specifically about cleanliness and maintenance because older hotel casinos can be a bit run down even if they look okay in photos. we were lucky our room was in good condition but some reviews mentioned issues with worn furniture and outdated bathrooms. dont just go by the fancy casino photos check room photos specifically.

MY FINAL VERDICT ON AUSTRALIAN HOTEL CASINOS

so after all that what do i actually think about these places. honestly theyre alright for what they are but not amazing. the rooms are decent quality and the convenience of having everything in one place is good. if you like gambling and entertainment then youll probably have a good time. but if youre not into that scene then theres not much point paying extra to stay at a hotel casino instead of a regular hotel.

the biggest issue for me is that the casino atmosphere dominates everything else. even if you dont gamble you still have to deal with the noise smoke and crowds which gets old pretty quick. i reckon hotel casinos should do better at separating the hotel and casino areas so guests can choose how much they want to engage with the gambling side of things.

would i stay at one again probably yeah if the price was right and i wanted entertainment options nearby. but i wouldnt go out of my way to choose a hotel casino over a regular hotel unless i specifically wanted to gamble or my family did. theyre a decent option for certain situations but not something im excited about tbh. just being real about it all.
