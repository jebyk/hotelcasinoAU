HOW CASINO HOTELS ARE DIFFERENT FROM NORMAL HOTELS

04 OCT 2025 | 8 MIN READ

okay so last month i stayed at crown perth with my family and honestly it was so different from like the holiday inn we usually go to and i started noticing heaps of stuff that made it way more interesting than just a regular hotel stay.

theres actually heaps of differences between staying at a regular hotel and a casino one and some of them surprised me so i thought id share what i noticed. like some things are obvious like theres pokies everywhere but other stuff is more subtle and actually kinda cool when you think about it.

whether youre thinking about staying at one of the big casino hotels in sydney or melbourne or just curious about what makes them tick this guide will break down all the major differences ive noticed. spoiler alert its not just about the gambling even though thats obviously a big part of it.

from the way theyre designed to keep you inside to the weird opening hours and the whole vibe of the place casino hotels are honestly their own category. lets get into it yeah.

THE LAYOUT IS DESIGNED TO CONFUSE YOU ON PURPOSE

this was the first thing i noticed when we checked in at crown. like trying to find the lifts to get to our room was actually confusing and i swear it wasnt an accident. regular hotels have pretty straightforward layouts right like reception is near the front door lifts are clearly marked and you can usually see where youre going.

casino hotels though they make it deliberately tricky to navigate. you have to walk through the gaming floor to get basically anywhere and theres no windows or clocks anywhere so you lose track of time. its actually pretty clever when you think about it from their perspective but annoying when youre just trying to find the bloody restaurant your mums booked for dinner.

the carpets are always these wild patterns too which apparently is intentional because it makes you focus on the bright flashy pokies instead of the floor. regular hotels have boring carpet that you dont even notice but casino hotels have these crazy geometric designs that honestly make me feel a bit dizzy sometimes.

another weird thing is how the ceilings are lower in the gaming areas which makes the space feel more intimate and keeps you focused on the games instead of looking around. then when you get to the hotel tower part the ceilings are suddenly way higher and it feels completely different. its like two separate buildings smooshed together.

EVERYTHING IS OPEN ALL NIGHT LITERALLY EVERYTHING

at a normal hotel if you want food at like 2am youre basically out of luck unless theres a vending machine. maybe room service if youre lucky but thats usually overpriced sandwiches that taste like cardboard. casino hotels though are completely different because people are gambling at all hours so everything stays open.

when i couldnt sleep one night i went down at like 3am and the food court was still going. not just open but actually busy with people eating and the staff werent even acting tired or annoyed. there were families having meals people getting coffee and the pokies were still going full blast with all the lights and sounds.

the gym was open too which was mental. like who works out at 4am but apparently heaps of people do at casino hotels. i saw this bloke on the treadmill watching the racing channel while he ran and i thought yeah that makes sense for this place. regular hotel gyms usually close at like 10pm if theyre even open at all.

even the concierge desk and reception are fully staffed all night. not just one bored person scrolling their phone but like actual full teams of staff ready to help. its honestly impressive how they keep everything running at full capacity around the clock. must cost them heaps in wages but i guess the pokies pay for it.

THE ROOMS ARE ACTUALLY NICER THAN REGULAR HOTELS

okay so this surprised me because i thought casino hotels would be all about the gaming floor and the rooms would be basic. but actually the rooms at crown were way nicer than most regular hotels ive stayed at. like properly nice not just fake fancy.

the bed was massive and super comfortable and the sheets were that high thread count stuff that feels really smooth. the bathroom had a separate shower and bath which you dont usually get unless youre staying somewhere expensive. there was a minibar that was fully stocked but we didnt touch it because my dad said itd cost like fifty bucks for a tiny bottle of water.

the view from our room was insane too. we could see the whole perth skyline and the river and at night all the lights looked really pretty. regular hotels usually give you a view of the car park or the building next door unless you pay extra. at the casino hotel our room was just randomly nice without us requesting anything special.

theres also way more tech in the rooms. like the tv was huge and had heaps of channels including the racing and sports betting channels obviously. the curtains were automatic which my little brother thought was the coolest thing ever. and there was this tablet thing where you could order room service or book spa treatments or whatever.

PRO TIP: even if youre not into gambling the rooms at casino hotels are usually better value than equivalent regular hotels because they make their money from the gaming floor not room rates. just dont get tempted by the pokies on your way to breakfast.

SECURITY IS NEXT LEVEL INTENSE

this is probably the biggest difference that you dont really notice at first but once you do its everywhere. casino hotels have security that makes regular hotels look like they have nobody watching anything. theres cameras absolutely everywhere and i mean everywhere.

my dad was telling me that casinos have some of the most sophisticated surveillance systems in the world because they need to watch for cheating and theft and stuff. apparently they can zoom in on a poker table from across the room and read the cards. bit creepy when you think about it but i guess it makes sense.

theres also way more security guards walking around. at a regular hotel you might see one person at reception but at crown there were security people everywhere. they were pretty chill and friendly actually but you could tell they were watching everything. one of them helped my mum find the toilets which was nice.

you also need your room key to access the lift to the hotel floors which regular hotels dont always have. and they check id at the entrance to the gaming floor to make sure youre over 18. regular hotels obviously dont need to do that because theres nothing age restricted in the building.

THE FOOD OPTIONS ARE ACTUALLY HEAPS BETTER

regular hotels usually have one restaurant and maybe a bar if youre lucky. continental breakfast in the morning thats the same everyday and if you want anything else youre ordering room service or going out. casino hotels though have so many food options its actually overwhelming.

crown perth had like fifteen different restaurants or something. theres the fancy ones where you need a reservation and dress nice then theres casual places and a food court and a buffet and heaps of bars. we tried the buffet one night and honestly it was really good. way better than those dodgy buffets you get at some pubs.

the variety is insane too. you can get asian food italian steakhouse whatever you want. and because everythings competing for customers the quality is usually pretty decent. at a regular hotel youre stuck with whatever theyve got and if you dont like it bad luck.

plus like i mentioned everything stays open late so you can get proper food at any time. not just reheated pizza or sad sandwiches but actual cooked meals. i had thai food at midnight one night and it was genuinely good. cant do that at a holiday inn.

DRINKS AND BARS EVERYWHERE

theres also bars absolutely everywhere in casino hotels. like you cant walk ten metres without passing another bar. some are fancy cocktail places others are just spots where you can grab a beer while you play the pokies. regular hotels usually have one bar that closes at like 11pm.

and the drinks are usually cheaper than you expect because they want you to stay and keep gambling. my dad got a beer for like seven bucks which is cheap for perth. at a regular hotel bar youd probably pay twelve or fifteen. obviously theyre hoping youll spend that money on the pokies instead but still.

THE ATMOSPHERE IS COMPLETELY DIFFERENT

regular hotels are pretty quiet right. people check in go to their rooms maybe use the pool and thats about it. the lobby is usually empty except for staff. casino hotels though are buzzing all the time. theres constantly heaps of people around and noise and activity.

the gaming floor is obviously the loudest part with all the pokies making noise and people cheering when they win and music playing. but even the other areas have this energy to them. the restaurants are busy the bars are packed and theres just this vibe like everyones having a good time.

it can be a bit much sometimes honestly. like when we first got there i was overwhelmed by how loud and bright everything was. but you get used to it pretty quick. and if you want quiet you just go back to your room which is usually in a completely separate part of the building and really peaceful.

the dress code is different too. at regular hotels people walk around in trackies and thongs and nobody cares. casino hotels have some areas where you need to dress properly. like we couldnt go to one of the nice restaurants because my brother was wearing shorts. nobody told us that before we tried to go in which was annoying.

LOYALTY PROGRAMS ARE WAY MORE SERIOUS

most hotels have loyalty programs where you get points or whatever but casino hotel programs are on another level. they really want you to come back and spend money so theyll give you heaps of benefits. my dad signed up for the crown rewards thing and immediately got offers for discounted rooms and free drinks.

if you gamble they track how much you spend and give you points based on that. apparently high rollers get insane perks like free suites and limo service and personal hosts who organize everything for them. regular hotel loyalty programs just give you a free breakfast or late checkout if youre lucky.

even if youre not gambling heaps you can still get benefits. we got a discount on the buffet and my mum got a voucher for the spa just for signing up. they really push the loyalty program hard because they know if they can get you coming back regularly theyll make way more money long term.

ENTERTAINMENT AND ACTIVITIES

regular hotels dont really have entertainment unless you count the pool. casino hotels though usually have heaps going on. crown had a theatre where they do shows and concerts. there was a comedy night while we were there that looked pretty good. regular hotels definitely dont have that.

theres also usually a spa and gym like i mentioned and sometimes shopping centers attached. crown had heaps of expensive shops selling jewelry and designer clothes and stuff. my mum loves that kind of thing so she was happy. regular hotels might have a gift shop that sells overpriced toothbrushes and tacky souvenirs.

some casino hotels in australia have nightclubs too which regular hotels obviously dont. the whole complex is designed to be this entertainment destination where you can stay for days without leaving. regular hotels are just places to sleep between doing other stuff.

THE PRICING MODEL IS TOTALLY DIFFERENT

heres something interesting that my dad explained to me. regular hotels make basically all their money from room rates so they charge as much as they can. casino hotels make most of their money from gambling so they can afford to charge less for rooms because they know theyll get it back from the pokies.

this means you can sometimes get really nice rooms at casino hotels for cheaper than average rooms at regular hotels. obviously they want you there so youll gamble but even if you dont gamble at all youre still getting a good deal on accommodation.

the catch is that everything else might be expensive. like the minibar and room service and spa treatments. they get you with the extras. but if youre smart about it and dont fall for that stuff you can stay somewhere really nice for not much money.

weekday rates are usually way cheaper too because the casino wants to fill rooms on slow nights. regular hotels dont have as much variation in pricing. so if you can go midweek instead of weekend you can save heaps.

FINAL THOUGHTS

so yeah after staying at crown and thinking about all these differences i reckon casino hotels are honestly pretty interesting. theyre not for everyone obviously like if you want a quiet relaxing stay somewhere peaceful a casino hotel probably isnt the best choice. but if you want entertainment and good food and a nice room for decent money theyre actually worth considering.

the main thing is just being aware of what youre getting into. dont expect a regular hotel experience because its completely different. expect noise and crowds and gambling everywhere and that deliberate confusing layout. but also expect better amenities more food options and rooms that are probably nicer than what youd get elsewhere for the same price.

if youre thinking about staying at one of the big australian casino hotels like crown in melbourne or perth or star in sydney just go in with realistic expectations. dont gamble more than you can afford to lose obviously. but enjoy the atmosphere and the facilities because thats what makes these places unique. theyre definitely different from normal hotels and in heaps of ways thats actually a good thing.
