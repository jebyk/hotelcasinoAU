THINGS I LEARNT ABOUT STAYING AT CASINO HOTELS

okay so like i stayed at a casino hotel for the first time last year with my family in queensland and honestly it was heaps different from regular hotels. like at first i thought it would just be a normal hotel with a casino downstairs or whatever but its actually way more complicated than that and theres so many things u need to know before u go.

i made heaps of mistakes on my first stay and some of them were pretty embarrassing tbh. but now that ive stayed at a few different casino hotels around australia i reckon i know enough to help other people avoid the same problems i had. so im gonna share all the stuff i learnt so u can have a better experience than i did.

the main thing is that casino hotels are designed differently from normal hotels and they have their own rules and culture that u need to understand. like theres stuff about noise and timing and dress codes and loyalty programs that nobody really tells u about until ur already there. so lets get into it.

THE NOISE SITUATION IS ACTUALLY INSANE

so the first thing i noticed was how loud everything is like all the time. the gaming floor is super noisy with all the pokies machines going off constantly and people yelling when they win and music playing everywhere. and the thing is that most casino hotels are designed so that the noise travels way more than u would expect.

when i stayed at my first casino hotel i made the mistake of booking the cheapest room which was like right above the gaming floor. huge mistake because i could hear everything until like 4am in the morning. the walls arent as thick as normal hotels because they want u to come downstairs and gamble instead of staying in ur room.

my advice is to always ask for a room on a higher floor or in a different section away from the gaming area. it costs a bit more sometimes but its totally worth it if u actually want to sleep. also bring earplugs just in case because even the quiet rooms can be noisier than regular hotels.

another thing is that casino hotels have people coming and going at all hours of the night. like in a normal hotel things quiet down after midnight but at a casino hotel people are checking in at 3am and walking through the hallways making noise. u just gotta be prepared for that.

TIME BECOMES REALLY WEIRD IN THERE

this is gonna sound weird but casino hotels are designed to make u lose track of time. theres no clocks anywhere on the gaming floor and the windows are usually covered or tinted so u cant tell if its day or night. its actually kind of creepy when u realise what theyre doing.

i remember walking around at like 2am thinking it was maybe 10pm because everything looked exactly the same as when i went downstairs at 8pm. the lighting is always the same bright artificial light and theres always the same amount of people around. its designed to keep u gambling longer without realising how much time has passed.

what helped me was setting alarms on my phone for different things like when to go back to my room or when to eat dinner. otherwise u can seriously lose track and spend way more time and money than u planned. also wearing a watch helps heaps because u cant just look around and figure out what time it is.

the restaurants and cafes inside casino hotels are also open at weird hours. like u can get a full breakfast at 2am or dinner at 11am which is actually pretty convenient but it also messes with ur normal routine. just be aware that ur sleep schedule might get completely messed up.

DRESS CODES ARE MORE IMPORTANT THAN U THINK

okay so i got turned away from a restaurant on my first night because i was wearing thongs and board shorts. i had no idea that casino hotels have actual dress codes for different areas. like the gaming floor is usually pretty casual but the nice restaurants and some of the bars have proper dress codes.

in australia most casino hotels require closed toe shoes and like proper pants or nice shorts for the restaurants. no thongs no singlets no ripped jeans in the fancy areas. i felt like an idiot standing there in my beach clothes while everyone else was dressed properly.

now i always pack at least one nice outfit even if im just planning to hang out by the pool most of the time. like bring proper shoes and a collared shirt for guys or a nice dress for girls. u never know when u might want to check out the fancy restaurant or go to a show.

also some casino hotels have different dress codes for different times of day. like the same restaurant might be casual during lunch but require smart casual at dinner. check the website before u go or call ahead to ask what the dress code is for the places u want to visit.

THE LOYALTY PROGRAMS ARE ACTUALLY WORTH IT

so i ignored the loyalty card thing at first because i thought it was just a scam to get my information but actually the rewards programs at casino hotels are way better than regular hotel loyalty programs. like u can get free stuff pretty quickly if u know how to use them.

when u sign up for a players card u get points not just for gambling but also for staying in the hotel eating at restaurants and using the spa and stuff. at some places in queensland and new south wales u can rack up enough points for a free night just from eating dinner and playing a few pokies.

the key is to always use ur card for everything even if ur not gambling much. swipe it when u check in swipe it before u eat swipe it at the bar. the points add up faster than u think and u can use them for free rooms free meals or even cash back at some places.

also if u sign up for the loyalty program before u book ur room u can sometimes get better rates or free upgrades. the casino wants u to be a member because then theyre more likely to get u back for future visits. so use that to ur advantage and always check the members rates before booking.

FOOD AND DRINKS ARE PRICED DIFFERENTLY

the pricing at casino hotels is really weird compared to normal places. like the food at the restaurants is usually pretty expensive but if ur gambling they might give u free drinks or meal vouchers. its all about whether ur spending money at the tables or pokies.

i learnt that u can often get free soft drinks and coffee while ur on the gaming floor even if ur only playing the minimum bet. just ask one of the waitresses walking around and theyll bring u stuff for free. at some places if ur playing for a while theyll even bring u free snacks or meal vouchers.

but if ur not gambling then everything is super expensive. like a sandwich that would cost ten dollars at a normal cafe might be twenty dollars at the casino hotel restaurant. they make up for the free stuff they give gamblers by charging heaps to everyone else.

my tip is to check if theres a food court or casual dining area because those are usually cheaper than the fancy restaurants. also look for buffet deals because sometimes the buffet is actually better value than ordering individual meals. and if u have a players card check if u have any dining credits or discounts available.

THE STAFF KNOW EVERYTHING ABOUT WHATS HAPPENING

casino hotel staff are way more helpful than regular hotel staff if u actually talk to them and ask questions. like they know all the secrets about when the restaurants are less busy when the gaming floor is quieter and what promotions are coming up.

i was struggling to find a quiet place to work on my laptop one morning and the front desk person told me about this lounge area that hardly anyone knows about. it was perfect and i never would have found it on my own. the staff also know which rooms are the best and might upgrade u if u just ask nicely.

dont be shy about asking for stuff because casino hotels are used to dealing with high rollers who demand things all the time. even if ur not spending heaps of money the staff are trained to be helpful and accommodating. the worst they can say is no but usually theyll try to help u out.

also make friends with the concierge because they can hook u up with tickets to shows discounts at restaurants and information about local attractions. they get commission for booking stuff so theyre motivated to help u have a good time. just tip them a bit if they do something really helpful.

SECURITY AND SAFETY STUFF U SHOULD KNOW

casino hotels have way more security than normal hotels which is good and bad. theres cameras everywhere and security guards walking around constantly. on one hand it feels pretty safe but on the other hand its kind of intense if ur not used to it.

u cant just wander around everywhere like u can in a normal hotel. some areas require u to show ur room key or players card to get in. the gaming floor usually has age restrictions so if ur under eighteen u cant even walk through that area to get to other parts of the hotel.

also be careful with ur belongings because casino hotels attract all kinds of people including some dodgy ones. dont leave ur stuff unattended even for a minute and use the room safe for valuables. i saw someone get their bag stolen right off a chair because they turned around for like two seconds.

if u win money gambling dont flash it around or make a big scene. just quietly cash out and put the money somewhere safe. the security is good but theres still opportunistic thieves who target people who just won big. be smart and keep a low profile.

BOOKING DIRECT VS THIRD PARTY WEBSITES

i used to always book hotels through those comparison websites but with casino hotels its actually better to book directly through their website or call them. the reason is that loyalty program benefits and upgrades usually only apply if u book direct.

when u book through a third party site the casino hotel doesnt have as much flexibility to give u upgrades or comps because theyre paying commission to the booking site. but if u book direct they can offer u better deals and throw in extras like free parking or dining credits.

also if something goes wrong with ur booking its way easier to fix if u booked direct. with third party sites u have to deal with their customer service and the hotel customer service and everyone blames each other. booking direct means u only have to deal with one company.

check the casino hotels website for package deals too because they often have promotions that include room and dining credits or show tickets. these packages are usually better value than booking everything separately and theyre only available if u book direct.

FINAL THOUGHTS ON CASINO HOTELS

so yeah staying at a casino hotel is definitely different from staying at a normal hotel but its not necessarily worse or better just different. if u know what to expect and how to work the system u can actually have a pretty good time and get decent value for money.

the main things to remember are to book a quiet room away from the gaming floor sign up for the loyalty program before u arrive bring proper clothes for the fancy areas and dont be afraid to ask staff for help. also watch ur budget because its really easy to spend way more than u planned when everything is designed to separate u from ur money.

personally i think casino hotels can be fun for a night or two but i wouldnt want to stay longer than that because the whole atmosphere is pretty intense. but if ur into gambling or u want to see some shows and eat at nice restaurants then casino hotels in australia offer pretty good facilities and entertainment options all in one place.

just go in with realistic expectations and a solid plan for how much ur willing to spend and ull probably have a good time. and remember that the house always wins in the long run so dont expect to pay for ur holiday with gambling winnings lol.
